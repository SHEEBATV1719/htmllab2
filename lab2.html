Late Sushant Singh Rajput Honored with Critics Choice Awards

Late Sushant Singh Rajput Honored at Dada Saheb Falke International Film Festival Awards With Critics Choice Awards

The Dada Saheb Falke International Film Festival Awards 2021 was held in Mumbai on Saturday and on the very evening late actor Sushant Singh Rajput was honoured with the Critics Choice Award at the ceremony. Dil Bechara actor, Who was a part of several goo films including M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story, Chhichhore and more, passed away tragically at his house in Mumbai on June 14, 2020. His demise shocked the nation, the fans and the entire Indian film industry.

  1. Born: 21 January 1986, Patna
  2. Died: 14 June 2020, Bandra West, Mumbai
  3. TV shows: Pavitra Rishta, Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil, Zara Nachke Dikha, Zee Cine Awards 2016
    4.

The actor began his career with TV Shows and rose to fame with the show, Pavitra Rishta with Ankita Lokhande. The actor’s death left everyone shocked and his last film was Dil Bechara with Sanjana Sanghi which was his last film. It was in 2013 with Abhishek Kapoor’s Kai Po Che! that Sushant made his big Bollywood debut and for his performance in the film based on Chetan Bhagat’s book ‘3 Mistakes of My Life,’ he won many awards and accolades.

Announcing the Honor, The Dadasaheb Falke Internation Film Festival Instagram handle Shared a photo of the actor and the honour that he had been bestowed with posthumously. The handle wrote, “Celebrating the dedication you’ve shown on the way to this achievement. Congratulations to Late @sushantsinghrajput for winning the Critic’s Best Actor award at Dadasaheb Phalke International Film Festival Awards 2021. We miss you!”
